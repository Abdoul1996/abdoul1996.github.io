About Me

Hi, I am Abdoulfatah Abdillahi (just call me Abdoul). I'm originally from East Africa, in Djibouti. I am a passionate Computer Science student who enjoys learning new things about technology and research methodologies.

I earned my BSc in Computer Science in December 2023 from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at San Francisco State University. I am currently pursuing my Master's in Data Science and AI, deepening my interdisciplinary skills and making impactful contributions at the intersection of computer science and biology.

My graduate thesis focuses on predicting E. coli drug resistance using traditional machine learning and deep learning models, under the guidance of Professor Dr. Pleuni Pennings (Biologist) and Dr. Sara El Alaouni (Computer Scientist).

I also completed an industry internship at Genentech in San Francisco, CA, where I worked as a bioinformatics researcher contributing to real-world biomedical data analysis.

Outside of academics, I enjoy running, playing soccer, and visiting historical places like Egypt and Ethiopia.

Work Experience

Graduate Student Researcher (Paid), San Francisco State University, CA

Jan 2024 – Present
  • Developed a pipeline to extract gene names from NCBI BLAST results and applied a 5% minor allele frequency threshold to filter SNP features across 15,695 genes, reducing dimensionality and enhancing feature quality for machine learning models.
  • Led a sub-team in developing and evaluating CNN and VAE models on a pangenome dataset to predict antibiotic resistance in E. coli, resulting in the acceptance of our work for presentation at the SACNAS 2024 conference.
  • Overcame gaps in drug resistance knowledge through collaboration with biology researchers, enhancing the biological relevance of machine learning models.

Bioinformatics Researcher, Genentech, San Francisco, CA

June 2023 - Sep 2023
  • Preprocessed and analyzed 150 synthetic patient trajectories simulating pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) responses across multiple dose levels by addressing stochastic variability and ensuring balanced train-test splits(70:30), enabling effective training of neural SDE models for treatment effect prediction.
  • Reduced data preprocessing time by implementing parallel processing across HPC nodes using job scheduling, significantly improving training throughput and enabling faster iteration cycles for neural SDE model development.

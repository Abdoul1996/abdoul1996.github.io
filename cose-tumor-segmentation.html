Multislice Multimodality Tumor Segmentation

COSE Conference Presentation - Computer Science Department

2023 SFSU College of Science & Engineering

Project Overview

This project focused on developing a U-Net model architecture to segment whole tumors utilizing multiple slices from T1, T2, T1CE and FLAIR modalities. We used the BraTS (Brain Tumor Segmentation) dataset to determine the best modality for whole tumor segmentation and implemented multi-class segmentation of brain tumors.

My Role & Contributions

  • Team Collaboration: Worked with Brandon Bahr Maravilla and Yoonji Lee
  • U-Net Implementation: Developed and optimized the neural network architecture
  • Multi-modal Analysis: Analyzed different imaging modalities (T1, T2, T1CE, FLAIR)
  • Dataset Processing: Worked with BraTS dataset for brain tumor segmentation
  • Performance Evaluation: Compared results across different modalities

Technical Implementation

Methodology

Our approach utilized advanced deep learning techniques for medical image analysis:

  • U-Net Architecture: Convolutional neural network designed for biomedical image segmentation
  • Multi-modal Processing: Integration of T1, T2, T1CE, and FLAIR MRI sequences
  • Multi-class Segmentation: Classification of different tumor regions
  • Cross-validation: Rigorous evaluation across different data splits

Technologies & Tools

Python TensorFlow/Keras U-Net Medical Imaging BraTS Dataset Computer Vision

Results & Impact

Key Achievements

  • Modality Comparison: Successfully determined the most effective imaging modality for tumor segmentation
  • Multi-class Segmentation: Implemented comprehensive tumor region classification
  • Team Efficiency: Optimized workflow with each team member focusing on specific modalities
  • Academic Presentation: Presented findings at SFSU COSE conference

Research Significance

This work contributes to the field of medical image analysis by providing insights into the effectiveness of different MRI modalities for brain tumor segmentation. The research has applications in clinical diagnosis and treatment planning.

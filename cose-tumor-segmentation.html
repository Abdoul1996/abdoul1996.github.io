Multislice Multimodality Tumor Segmentation

COSE Conference Presentation - Computer Science Department

2023 SFSU College of Science & Engineering

Problem Statement

Brain tumor segmentation from medical imaging is crucial for accurate diagnosis and treatment planning. Traditional approaches often rely on single imaging modalities, which may not capture the full complexity of tumor characteristics. The challenge lies in developing robust segmentation methods that can effectively utilize multiple MRI sequences to improve accuracy and reliability.

Solution Approach

We developed a comprehensive deep learning framework using U-Net architecture to perform multislice multimodality tumor segmentation. Our approach integrates T1, T2, T1CE, and FLAIR MRI sequences from the BraTS dataset to achieve superior segmentation performance through multi-modal analysis and multi-class classification.

Technical Implementation

Methodology

Our research employed state-of-the-art deep learning techniques specifically designed for medical image analysis:

  • U-Net Architecture: Convolutional neural network with encoder-decoder structure optimized for biomedical image segmentation
  • Multi-modal Processing: Simultaneous integration of T1-weighted, T2-weighted, T1CE (contrast-enhanced), and FLAIR MRI sequences
  • Multi-class Segmentation: Classification of tumor regions into enhancing tumor, non-enhancing tumor, and peritumoral edema
  • Cross-validation Strategy: Rigorous evaluation using k-fold cross-validation to ensure model generalizability
  • Data Augmentation: Implementation of rotation, flipping, and intensity variations to increase dataset diversity

Dataset & Preprocessing

  • BraTS Dataset: Brain Tumor Segmentation Challenge dataset with multi-modal MRI scans
  • Image Preprocessing: Normalization, skull stripping, and co-registration across modalities
  • Slice Selection: Multi-slice approach utilizing 2D slices from 3D volumes
  • Quality Control: Manual verification of segmentation masks and data integrity

Technologies & Tools

Python TensorFlow/Keras U-Net Medical Imaging BraTS Dataset Computer Vision NumPy OpenCV

Results & Impact

Key Achievements

  • Modality Comparison: Successfully evaluated and compared the effectiveness of T1, T2, T1CE, and FLAIR sequences for tumor segmentation
  • Multi-class Segmentation: Implemented comprehensive classification of tumor subregions (enhancing tumor, non-enhancing tumor, peritumoral edema)
  • Performance Optimization: Achieved improved segmentation accuracy through multi-modal fusion compared to single-modality approaches
  • Team Collaboration: Successfully coordinated research efforts across team members with specialized focus areas
  • Academic Contribution: Presented research findings at SFSU COSE conference, contributing to the computer science research community

Research Significance

This research contributes significantly to the field of medical image analysis by providing empirical evidence for the effectiveness of multi-modal MRI approaches in brain tumor segmentation. The findings have direct applications in clinical settings for:

  • Diagnostic Accuracy: Improved tumor detection and characterization
  • Treatment Planning: Enhanced precision for surgical and radiation therapy planning
  • Patient Outcomes: Better prognosis assessment and treatment monitoring
  • Research Foundation: Establishing baseline for future multi-modal segmentation studies

Future Directions

The research opens several avenues for future investigation, including 3D segmentation approaches, real-time processing capabilities, and integration with clinical workflow systems.

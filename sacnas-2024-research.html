Pangenome Pipeline for Predicting Antibiotic Resistance in E. coli

SACNAS 2024 Conference Presentation

October 2024 SACNAS National Conference

Project Overview

Presented a poster on behalf of my research team, showcasing our pipeline for integrating multiple E. coli datasets into a unified pangenome framework and applying machine learning models to predict antibiotic resistance. This comprehensive approach combines data from Mills, Gladstone, and Kalloneen datasets to create a robust prediction system.

My Role & Contributions

  • Poster Presentation: Presented our team's research findings at SACNAS 2024 conference
  • Pipeline Development: Contributed to the integration of multiple E. coli datasets
  • Pangenome Framework: Worked on creating unified pangenome framework for analysis
  • Team Collaboration: Collaborated with CoDE Lab researchers on comprehensive approach

Technical Implementation

Methodology

Our pangenome pipeline approach involved:

  • Dataset Integration: Combined Mills (2000 samples), Gladstone (3000 samples), and Kalloneen (2000 samples) datasets
  • Pangenome Creation: Built unified pangenome framework from multiple E. coli genomes
  • Feature Extraction: Identified SNPs, gene presence, and gene absence patterns
  • Machine Learning Models: Applied logistic regression, random forest, and gradient boosted decision trees

Technologies & Tools

Python Pangenome Analysis Machine Learning Data Integration Bioinformatics Statistical Analysis

Results & Impact

Key Achievements

  • Comprehensive dataset integration combining 7,000+ E. coli samples from three major datasets
  • Unified pangenome framework for enhanced accuracy and generalizability
  • Conference presentation at prestigious SACNAS 2024 conference
  • Team collaboration with CoDE Lab researchers at SFSU

Research Significance

This work addresses limitations of traditional antibiotic resistance testing by providing a comprehensive pangenome-based approach. The pipeline integrates multiple datasets to enhance accuracy and generalizability compared to traditional gene presence/absence or SNP-only methods.
